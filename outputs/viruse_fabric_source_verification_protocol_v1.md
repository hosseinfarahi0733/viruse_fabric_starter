# Source Verification Protocol v1

## Status

Scope: draft-source-verification-protocol-no-new-citations-no-claim-validation

This artifact creates a source verification protocol only. It does not perform a literature search, does not add citations, does not add references, does not verify sources yet, does not claim literature support, does not claim validation, and does not claim manuscript readiness.

## Purpose

The purpose of this protocol is to define how future sources may be searched, screened, verified, and assigned to bounded support roles before they are allowed into a related-work section for VF-H2.

VF-H2 remains a safe abstract toy hypothesis about memory-ledger-driven propagation over finite graph-like state spaces. Literature may be used only for conceptual positioning, methodological framing, terminology support, or background. Literature must not be used as validation evidence for VF-H2 or for the broader Viruse Fabric theory.

## Verification Principle

No source may be cited until all of the following are recorded:

1. exact bibliographic metadata,
2. source type,
3. literature bucket,
4. exact claim supported,
5. exact claim not supported,
6. allowed use,
7. forbidden use,
8. overextension risk,
9. source quality status,
10. verification decision.

## Candidate Source Types

Allowed future source types:

- peer-reviewed research article,
- peer-reviewed survey or review article,
- academic book or monograph,
- conference paper from a reputable venue,
- methodological paper,
- foundational paper,
- official documentation only when documenting a tool or method.

Disallowed source types for theory support:

- blog post,
- unsourced web page,
- marketing page,
- unverifiable preprint used as settled evidence,
- popular article used as technical support,
- source that requires biological or operational claims,
- source that encourages real-world pathogen, receptor, host-range, infectivity, or immune-evasion interpretation.

## Literature Buckets for Future Verification

### 1. Graph Dynamical Systems

Allowed role:
Conceptual positioning for graph-based state updates.

Required verification:
A future source must actually discuss graph dynamical systems, discrete graph state updates, or networked dynamical processes.

Forbidden use:
Do not use this bucket to claim VF-H2 validation or equivalence to an existing model.

### 2. Memory and Non-Markovian Processes in Networks

Allowed role:
Conceptual framing for history-dependent dynamics.

Required verification:
A future source must explicitly discuss memory, history-dependence, non-Markovian dynamics, or path dependence in network processes.

Forbidden use:
Do not use this bucket to claim that memory literature proves VF-H2.

### 3. Memory-Augmented Automata and Rule-Based Toy Systems

Allowed role:
Conceptual positioning for discrete rule-based systems with memory-like state.

Required verification:
A future source must discuss cellular automata, graph automata, automata with memory, or rule-based toy systems.

Forbidden use:
Do not claim formal equivalence unless a separate proof exists.

### 4. Reservoir-Style Memory and Recurrent State Analogies

Allowed role:
Analogy-level positioning for systems where prior state affects future behavior.

Required verification:
A future source must discuss reservoir computing, echo state networks, recurrent state dynamics, or memory capacity.

Forbidden use:
Do not claim VF-H2 is a reservoir computing model.

### 5. Temporal Networks and Multi-Time Indexing

Allowed role:
Conceptual framing for time-indexed graph or network processes.

Required verification:
A future source must discuss temporal networks, dynamic graphs, time-varying networks, or multi-time process structure.

Forbidden use:
Do not claim empirical validation of the three-time coordinate.

### 6. Abstract Causal Structure and Causal-Mass Language

Allowed role:
Terminology and conceptual caution around abstract causal dependencies.

Required verification:
A future source must discuss abstract causal structure, causal graphs, partial-order dynamics, or discrete causal ordering.

Forbidden use:
Do not claim physical causal-set support or real-world causality.

### 7. Null Models, Ablation, Robustness, and Artifact Checks

Allowed role:
Methodological framing for toy-model evaluation.

Required verification:
A future source must discuss null models, ablation studies, robustness checks, simulation artifacts, reproducibility, or independent implementation checks.

Forbidden use:
Do not claim that passing checks proves VF-H2.

### 8. Artificial Life, Complex Systems, and Toy-Model Positioning

Allowed role:
Position VF-H2 as a toy model in abstract complex systems style.

Required verification:
A future source must discuss artificial life, complex systems toy models, abstract simulation models, or mechanism-oriented toy modeling.

Forbidden use:
Do not imply biological relevance or operational applicability.

## Claim-Support Levels

Allowed support levels:

1. background only,
2. terminology support only,
3. conceptual positioning only,
4. methodological framing only.

Forbidden support levels:

1. VF-H2 validation,
2. Viruse Fabric theory validation,
3. formal proof,
4. empirical validation,
5. biological validation,
6. external validation,
7. manuscript readiness,
8. submission readiness.

## Source Acceptance Rules

A source may be accepted only if:

- it matches one of the planned literature buckets,
- it supports a clearly bounded claim,
- the exact supported claim is written down,
- the unsupported claims are written down,
- it does not force real biological or operational interpretation,
- it does not expand the claim beyond safe abstract toy positioning,
- it can be cited without implying validation.

## Source Rejection Rules

A source must be rejected if:

- it is used only because the title sounds relevant,
- it cannot be inspected sufficiently,
- it is not actually about the assigned bucket,
- it encourages biological or operational extrapolation,
- it is being used to imply validation,
- it is being used to imply formal proof,
- it is being used to imply empirical support,
- it is being used to imply manuscript readiness.

## Citation Insertion Gate

A future citation may be inserted only after:

1. source metadata is recorded,
2. source bucket is assigned,
3. exact claim support is bounded,
4. forbidden uses are listed,
5. overextension risk is assessed,
6. no validation claim is introduced,
7. no real-bio boundary is crossed,
8. the source is approved for related-work use.

## Explicit Non-Claims

This protocol does not claim:

- source verification has been performed,
- citations have been added,
- references have been added,
- literature support has been established,
- literature validation has been established,
- theory validation,
- formal proof,
- external validation,
- independent empirical validation,
- biological relevance,
- real-world relevance,
- manuscript readiness,
- submission readiness.

## Safety Boundary

Safe abstract toy model only. No real biological datasets, no real pathogen models, no receptor parameters, no operational targeting, no wet-lab protocol, no infectivity optimization, no immune evasion optimization, and no host range prediction are introduced.
